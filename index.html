Inequality Of Wealth In America

Stirling Waite (stirlingw@gmail.com), Jee Loong Chew (jee.loong@gmail.com), Kirill B (kirill00k@gmail.com)

Abstract:

Our perception of the distribution of wealth in America might not be accurate, especially in terms of inequalities and the actual real numbers.

Are only the top 10% earners in the economy living the “life of luxury”, and the other 90% getting the scraps? How much money does it take to “feel rich” in America? In this web app we look at the bottom 90%, top 10%, top 1%, and the top 400 income per household's in America.

Motivation:

Use Case:

You are a college graduate, with a couple years of experience under your belt. You are earning a low six figure income. The money you are earning is more than most of your friends and family, yet you still don’t “feel rich”. You are curious to see how your income stacks up against the rest of the United States.

After a Google search you find a website with the title “The Inequality of Wealth in America”. It says that says it can help you find how you rate income wise vs the rest of America. Also, it provides other general information about household income in America. You click on the site to see more information.

Questions this study tries to solve:

  • Are only the top 10% earners in the economy living the “life of luxury”?
  • How much money does it take to “feel rich” in America?
  • Where is all wealth accumulated?

Quick Info:

  • University of California at Berkeley economist Emmanuel Saez estimates that between 2009 and 2012, the top 1 percent captured 95 percent of total income growth.
  • If your income around $98,000 a year, you are in the top 25%
  • If you income around $155,000 at year, you are in the top 10%
  • The top 1 percent of Americans owns 40 percent of the nation’s wealth
  • The top 1 percent of Americans take home 24 percent of national income
  • The top 1 percent of Americans own half of the country’s stocks, bonds, and mutual funds

*source: CNN Money and Business Insider
